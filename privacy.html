Privacy Policy

This policy explains what Shade Line stores, what it does not collect, and how challenge links work.

Play the game →

Effective date

This Privacy Policy applies to Shade Line as made available through this website as of April 1, 2026.

Overview

Shade Line is designed to be lightweight and privacy-conscious. The game does not require you to create an account, submit personal details, or provide payment information in order to play.

In its current form, Shade Line primarily uses your own browser to store limited gameplay information locally on your device so the site can remember things like your best score.

What we store locally on your device

Shade Line uses browser local storage to save a small amount of gameplay data directly in your browser. This may include your personal best score and the number of times the game has been played on that browser.

This local storage data stays on your device and is not, by itself, transmitted to us through the game interface. You can clear it at any time by clearing your browser’s site data or local storage for this website.

What we do not ask you to provide

Shade Line does not ask you to create a user account. It does not ask for your name, email address, mailing address, phone number, or payment details in order to play the game.

The site is also not designed to collect private messages, uploaded files, or profile data from players.

Challenge links

Shade Line allows players to generate challenge links that include a score in the URL, such as a score to beat. That score is part of the link itself and can be seen by anyone who receives or opens the link.

Challenge links are intended to share game results, not personal identity. Please avoid adding personal information to messages you send alongside a challenge link.

Analytics, cookies, and server logs

Shade Line does not intentionally use gameplay accounts or in-game forms to collect personal data. If analytics tools, hosting logs, or similar infrastructure services are added by the site operator or hosting provider, those services may process technical information such as IP address, browser type, device type, referring pages, and timestamps in the ordinary course of delivering and securing the website.

If such services are introduced in production, this Privacy Policy should be updated to reflect the specific providers and purposes involved.

Children’s privacy

Shade Line is a general-audience game and is not intended to knowingly collect personal information from children. Because the game does not require account registration or direct submission of personal details, the amount of personal data involved is intentionally limited.

Data retention

Information stored in your browser remains there until it is deleted by you, your browser, or your device settings. Any technical logs retained by a hosting provider, if applicable, would be governed by that provider’s retention practices.

Your choices

You can stop using the game at any time. You can remove locally stored gameplay data by clearing your browser storage for this site. You can also choose not to use challenge links if you do not want your score included in a shareable URL.

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect product changes, legal requirements, or operational updates. When the policy changes, the updated version should be posted on this page with a revised effective date when appropriate.

Contact

If you operate Shade Line in production, you should add a real contact email or support method here so users can ask privacy-related questions. Until then, this page is intended to describe the current behavior of the site as accurately as possible.
